ITINERARY:

Very early we will transfer from your hotel to the train station in Ollantaytambo, providing you with a personalized service as we handle small groups. It is here where we will board the train that passes through the Sacred Valley of the Incas, on this route you will enjoy beautiful landscapes until we reach the town of Aguas Calientes. In Aguas Calientes we can take the bus that will take us to the Archaeological Sanctuary of Machu Picchu. The trip takes 30 minutes, finally we will arrive at the Inca citadel, you will be amazed at this monument considered one of the 7 wonders of the Modern World, immediately our guide will start the guided tour so that you know in a better way the history and architecture of the Inca City from Machu Picchu. After the guided tour, you will have time to freely tour Machu Picchu. In the afternoon we finish the 1 day tour by boarding the return train and we will pick you up from the station to transfer you to your hotel in Cusco.

MACCHU PICCHU:

From southern Quechua machu pikchu ("Old Mountain") is the contemporary name given to a stone llaqta (ancient Inca town) built mainly in the mid-15th century on the rocky promontory that joins the Machu Picchu and Huayna Picchu mountains in the eastern slope of the Central Andes, south of Peru. Its original name would have been Picchu or Picho. According to documents from the mid-sixteenth century, Machu Picchu would have been one of the rest residences of Pachac√∫tec (first Inca emperor, 1438-1470). However, some of its best constructions and the evident ceremonial character of the main access road to the llaqta would show that it was used as a religious sanctuary. Both uses, the palace and the sanctuary, would not have been incompatible. Some experts seem to have ruled out, on the other hand, an alleged military character, so the popular qualifiers of "fortress" or "citadel" could have been surpassed
